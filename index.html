    Interesting facts about Origami:-
    Origami was derived from 2 Japanese Words- Ori(folded) Kami(paper).
    Before that, the art was called "Orikata".
    An ancient Japanese legend says that if you fold one thousand cranes you will be granted a wish.

Camels

Camels can reach 7 feet in

height and weigh up to 1500 pounds.

Their eyes have 3 eyelids and 2

layers of eyelashes that

prevent sand to go in their eyes.

Chameleon

Chameleon's feet work like salad tongues.

They vary wildly in terms of size.

Skin crystals enable them to change color at will.

Unlike many lizards chameleons

can't regrow their tails.

Pigeons

Pigeons might be the first

domesticated birds. They won over

Charles Darwin and Nikola Tesla.

They understand space and time.

They can find their way back to the nest

from 1300 miles away.

Teddy Bear

The Oxford English Dictionary dates

the first use of the term to 1906.

In 1902 in Germany Steiff launched

"Bear 55 PB" the first

toy bear with jointed arms and legs.

Panda

Giant Panda can actually climb trees and

can also swim. Pandas go from pink to

black and white. A baby panda weighs equal to an apple.

An adult panda can eat 12-38 kilos per day.

Flying Cicada

There are about 3000 species of cicada according to

National Geographic. They vary in size from

0.75cm to 2.25 cm in length. Cicadas can be black,

brown or green and can have red, white or blue eyes.
